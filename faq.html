Basic Information & Common Questions



❖ Question: Do you offer any RN or LPN courses?

Answer: We only offer CNA/LNA classes as well as CPR. We do not offer RN or LPN classes.

❖ Question: Do you offer any payment plans?

Answer: We offer a variety of payment plans that meet most students' needs. Students need to contact administration to arrange payment plans. Please note that we do not accept personal checks.

The total cost of the program is $1,250 per candidate.
• Enrollment/Registration is $300.
• Tuition is $950.

We do accept installment payments for tuition weekly or bi-weekly. After graduation, preparation for the board-exam is free. We do not charge for textbooks, equipment, or additional lab use.

❖ Question: Do you offer Financial Aid?

Answer: We do not offer any federally funded financial assistance, however, students may obtain financial assistance through an external agency. Some well-known resources are listed below:

Historically, students have received funding from sources such as their employer, credit unions, religious organizations, community programs, but mostly family/friends. Your personal drive and determination will likely be the ultimate contributor to your success. Please do not hesitate to call or come in and speak with us if you have any questions. Our number is (602) 935-6628, we'll do our best to help.

❖ Question: How do I find transportation to school?

Answer: The school is conveniently located along city bus routes on Central Avenue & Dunlap Road. Please see the map on this page and on the home page to view our location. Students have had success using the valley metro trip planner available here.

❖ Question: How do I get books?

Answer: Books are available to all students as loans for the duration of the program. Laboratory materials/tools or extra hours needed throughout the training program are also available at no additional charge.
